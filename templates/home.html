Shariah Compliant (Halal) Guidelines

In Islamic finance, for a company to be eligible for investment, it must pass a strict two-step screening protocol:

Step 1: Halal Business Activities

The core business must operate in ethical and permissible sectors such as technology, software, healthcare, construction, retail, and agriculture.

Step 2: Balance Sheet Integrity

To ensure financial health and avoid interest transactions (Riba), the company must stay below three balance sheet thresholds relative to its **Total Assets**:

  • Interest Debt must be less than 33%
  • Cash & Interest Investments must be less than 33%
  • Accounts Receivable must be less than 49%
Step 3: Purification Threshold

Incidental non-halal business revenue plus interest income must not exceed 5% of total revenue. Any interest income generated must be donated to charity.

Prohibited (Haram) Activities

A company is immediately flagged as Non-Shariah compliant if it participates in prohibited business operations or breaches ratios:

Forbidden Industries

Conventional Interest-Bearing Finance, Conventional Insurance, Gambling & Casinos, Pork & Non-Halal Food processing, Weapons & Armaments, Alcohol, Tobacco, and Adult Entertainment.

Excessive Leverage & Debt

Relying on conventional loans where the debt exceeds 33% of assets violates the prohibition of interest (Riba) and disqualifies the security.

Excessive Cash Accumulation

Holding interest-earning bank deposits or conventional bonds that exceed 33% of assets indicates that the company is trading on cash, which is prohibited.
